### About Me

I am **Soomin Kwon**, a master's student in the **Department of Statistics and Data Science at Inha University**, Republic of Korea, and a researcher affiliated with the **Probability and Recognition Lab (PCRL)**.

My research interests lie in **generative AI, diffusion models, image generation, computer vision, and multimodal intelligent systems**. I am particularly interested in developing robust and practically effective generative modeling frameworks for real-world applications in defense, scientific sensing, and intelligent decision-support systems.

My recent and ongoing research includes:

- **Diffusion-Transformer Hybrid Framework for Dataset Construction in Deep Learning-Based TEL Detection from Satellite Imagery**
- **HDD-CMS: Structure-Conditioned Diffusion with Hybrid Noise and Dynamic Optimization for Library-Complementary Mass Spectrometry**
- **Research on Improving Rectified Flow for Stable and Efficient Generative Modeling**
- **Research on Applying the Frequency-Aware Prior Framework to DDPM under Class-Imbalanced Settings**

My broader research interests include:

- Generative AI and image synthesis  
- Diffusion models and flow-based generative models  
- Vision-Language Models (VLM)  
- Scientific AI for hazardous gas analysis and sensing systems
